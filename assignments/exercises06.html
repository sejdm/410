Exercise sheet 6

  1. Prove that every map from a simply connected space to \(S^1\) is null homotopic.

  2. Prove that every map from \(\mathbb{RP}^2\) to \(S^1\) is null homotopic.

  3. Prove that if \(X\) has a simply connected cover, then given any point in \(x \in X\), there exists a neighourhood \(U\) of \(x\) so that \(i_* : \pi_1(U, x) \to \pi_1(X, x)\) is trivial (here, \(i_*\) is the homomorphism induced by the inclusion map \(i: U \to X\)).

  4. Prove that any two universal coverings are equivalent.

  5. Prove that the “Hawaiian earring” is not semi-locally simply connected.

  6. Find all path-connected coverings of \(S^1\) and \(S^1 \times S^1\) up to equivalence.
